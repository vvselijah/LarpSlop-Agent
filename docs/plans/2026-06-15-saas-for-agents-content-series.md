## The thesis (one sentence, his voice)

> "There are about to be more AI agents online than people — and almost nobody is building software
> for them. Everyone's still selling to humans. The first people to sell to the AGENTS win the next
> ten years."

**Guardrail (from the feasibility doc — do NOT violate in any script):**
- Do NOT state "more agents than humans" as present-day FACT. The defensible version is the forward
  one: **IDC projects 1B+ deployed AI agents by 2029**, and they're starting to *buy* software, not
  just use it. Frame as trajectory, not today's headcount.

  events below are stronger — lead with those.

## Part 1 — "Your favorite software company is quietly dying. Here's the receipt."

**Niche:** AI/Tech × Money (his two strongest). **Format:** reel, hook-gate first 1.5s.

**HOOK (say this, caps = energy):**
> "In February 2026, the stock market wiped out $285 BILLION of software companies in a single
> selloff. They even gave it a name — the SaaSpocalypse. And it wasn't a recession. It was ROBOTS."

**PAYOFF (the turn):**
> Here's what actually happened. For 20 years, software companies charged you 'per seat' — pay us per
> human who logs in. Then AI agents showed up and did the work of the humans. Monday.com replaced its
> ENTIRE 100-person sales-dev team with agents in January 2026 — response time went from 24 hours to
> 3 minutes. Atlassian reported its first-ever DROP in paid seats. When one agent replaces ten logins,
> 'per seat' pricing doesn't just shrink — it falls apart. Per-seat went from 21% of SaaS to 15% in
> twelve months. That's the whole game changing in real time.

**CTA:** "Follow — I'm breaking down where the money goes next, because somebody's getting rich off
this." (Part 2.)

**Verified facts used:** $285B Feb-2026 SaaS selloff ("SaaSpocalypse"); Monday.com replaced 100-person
SDR team Jan 2026 (24h → 3min); Atlassian first-ever seat-count decline driven by agent adoption;
per-seat pricing 21% → 15% in 12 months. [Taskade, Korix/DEV, SoftwareSeni]

## Part 2 — "Software is about to charge you for RESULTS, not seats."

**Niche:** Money / Business model. **Format:** reel or carousel (pricing comparison = great saves bait).

**HOOK:**
> "Soon you won't pay for software at all. You'll pay it like an EMPLOYEE — only when it actually gets
> the job done. And the companies who figured this out first are already winning."

**PAYOFF:**
> This is called outcome-based pricing and it's already live. Intercom charges 99 cents every time
> its AI actually RESOLVES a support conversation — not per seat, per RESULT. HubSpot undercut them to
> 50 cents in April 2026. Think about how wild that is: the software only makes money when it works.
> That's not a subscription, that's a commission. The model that built the entire SaaS industry —
> 'pay us monthly per person' — is getting replaced by 'pay us per outcome.' If you run a business,
> stop buying seats. Start buying outcomes.

**CTA:** "Save this — it'll change how you buy every tool you use." 

**Verified facts used:** Intercom $0.99 per resolved conversation; HubSpot dropped to $0.50 in April
2026; hybrid (seat + usage) now ~41% of SaaS, the new default; per-resolution pricing aligns
vendor/buyer incentives. [HighRadius, Korix/DEV, Getmonetizely]

## Part 3 — "The customer isn't a person anymore. It's a bot. And bots buy DIFFERENTLY."

**Niche:** AI/Tech × Founder. **Format:** reel (this is the THESIS part — the most "smart" one).

**HOOK:**
> "Everything you've ever been sold was designed to persuade a HUMAN. Pretty website, good copy,
> nice colors. None of that works on your next customer — because your next customer is a robot, and
> robots don't care about your branding."

**PAYOFF:**
> Here's the shift nobody's talking about. A human customer wants persuasion — make me feel something,
> make it look good. An AGENT customer wants the opposite: structure, capability, permission, trust,
> and clean data. It doesn't read your landing page — it reads your API. It doesn't get talked into
> anything — it checks if you're machine-readable and moves on. So every company built to charm humans
> has a problem: the buyer changed, and the buyer can't be charmed. The winners aren't the ones with
> the prettiest app. They're the ones with the cleanest data and the most open API. The UI moat is
> dead. The DATA moat is everything.

**CTA:** "This is the biggest shift in tech right now and barely anyone sees it. Part 4: how agents
actually pay for things — yes, they have their own wallets now."

**Verified facts used:** "SaaS isn't dead, the easy-UI moat is dead — clean data/API backends win"
(sober counter-take). The persuasion-vs-structure framing is Elijah's own vault insight, flagged in
the feasibility doc as the genuinely sharp, well-supported core. 40% of enterprise apps will embed
task-specific agents by end of 2026 (up from <5% in 2024). [PANews/BuiltIn via feasibility doc;
Grandview/Cyntexa]

## Part 4 — "AI agents now have their own credit cards. Seriously."

**Niche:** AI/Tech × Money. **Format:** reel (the "wait, WHAT" novelty hook = high reach).

**HOOK:**
> "There's a payment system you've never heard of that already moved 165 MILLION transactions — and
> not a single one was made by a human. Agents are out here SPENDING MONEY, on their own, right now."

**PAYOFF:**
> In the last year the biggest names in money quietly built rails so AI agents can buy things by
> themselves. Google shipped a protocol called AP2 — Agent Payments Protocol — where an agent gets a
> cryptographically signed 'mandate' to spend on your behalf. Coinbase's x402 payment rail processed
> roughly 165 million agent transactions in its first months. Adobe measured a 4,700% jump in
> AI-driven traffic to shopping sites in a single year. This isn't a someday thing. The plumbing for
> a machine economy already got laid. The question isn't IF agents become customers — they already
> are. The question is whether your business is even set up to take their money.

**CTA:** "Final part: the actual opportunity — what to build while everyone else is asleep. Follow so
you don't miss it."

**Verified facts used:** Google AP2 (Agent Payments Protocol; mandates = W3C Verifiable Credentials,
signed claims); Coinbase x402 ~165M agent transactions in first months; Adobe 4,700% YoY jump in
generative-AI traffic to US retail sites (Jul 2024 → Jul 2025); MCP donated to Linux Foundation's
Agentic AI Foundation Dec 2025 as the tool-discovery layer agents sit on. [AP2-protocol.org,
Eco/AP2 guide, Adobe via Eco/Descope]

> Look at the numbers. The agentic-AI market is about $11 billion in 2026 and projected past $180

> Translation: agents aren't coming, they're already the users — and almost nobody is building FOR
> them. You don't have to out-build Google or Stripe. You just have to be early in one niche they
> haven't touched, with clean data and an open door. The internet got rebuilt for humans once. It's
> getting rebuilt for agents right now. Be early — that's the whole move. Jesus is how, but hustle
> is the vehicle. Get to work.

**CTA:** "If this opened your eyes, send it to one founder who's still building for 2015. Follow for
the AI shift nobody's explaining."

**Verified facts used:** Agentic-AI market ~$10.86–10.91B (2026), projected ~$182.97B by 2033 at
